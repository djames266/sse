Email Verifier:

How does it work?

The verifier tool is based on Secure/Multipurpose Internet Mail Extensions (S/MIME) technology (mainly digital signatures).
Before you receive an email message from the website, digital signing is performed on the email which results in the
codes you see at the bottom of your received message separated by ---+++++++---. These codes capture the original
data as intended by the sender. When you copy the received message into the message field, the verifier decodes
the received digital signatures and compares it to the received message data. If they match, it means the data received
matches the original data sent and can be trusted (verifier results in a pass). Otherwise, the data has been altered during
transit and should not be trusted (verifier results in a fail).

Application (a simple defense against phishing)

Phishing is the fraudulent practice of sending emails claiming to be from a genuine organisation. These emails are
designed to steal personal identity and information. Say this website sends you an email asking you to update personal
information. If you were unsure about this email, you could use this tool to verify if it was truly sent by the website.

How do I use it?

Simply copy the whole email message received, paste it in the message field and click check.
